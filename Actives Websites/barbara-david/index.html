About

I'm Barbara David, a junior at Indiana University studying Cybersecurity and Global Policy—a joint degree between the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering and the Hamilton Lugar School of Global and International Studies.

The program combines hands-on technical skills with policy, law, and international perspectives, which is why I chose it. I'm interested in how security, privacy, and diplomacy shape the digital world we live in.

Why This Field Matters

01

Technical Foundation

Cybersecurity isn’t just policy—it’s built on programming, data structures, networked systems, and security protocols. Understanding how systems work is essential to protecting them.

02

Policy & Law

Privacy, regulation, and international norms shape how we defend and govern digital infrastructure. Policy decisions directly affect who gets protected and how.

03

Global Impact

Cyber threats cross borders. Effective security requires understanding cultural, economic, and diplomatic contexts—not just firewalls and encryption.

Focus Areas in the Major

  • Network and systems security
  • Privacy and data protection
  • International relations and cyber diplomacy
  • Legal and ethical dimensions of cybersecurity
  • Critical infrastructure and national security
