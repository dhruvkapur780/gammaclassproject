Philosophy

How I think about data

1. Every data point is a person

My experience as a lifeguard taught me that each individual in a crowd carries their own story, risks, and needs. I bring that same mindset to data: a row in a dataset represents a life, not an abstraction. Ethical data work begins by honoring the people behind the numbers.

2. Clarity is a moral duty

In both emergency situations and analytics, confusion costs time—and sometimes much more. I believe that clear models, transparent assumptions, and honest communication are not just technical skills, but ethical commitments to the people who rely on our insights.

3. Data is a guide, not a dictator

Data can reveal patterns we could never see alone, but it cannot tell us what we ought to value. I aim to use data as a disciplined guide for better decisions while keeping human judgment, context, and compassion at the center.

“The beginning of wisdom is the definition of terms.”

— Socrates

In data science, defining our terms—features, labels, and success metrics—is where our ethics quietly enter the model.
